ARTICLE 1: SCOPE OF WORK
================================================================================

1.1 Work Description

Subcontractor shall furnish all labor, materials, equipment, and supervision 
necessary to complete the following electrical scope of work:

Division 26 - Electrical:
- Complete electrical distribution system including main switchgear (4000A, 480V)
- Emergency generator connection and automatic transfer switch
- All branch circuit wiring for commercial and residential units
- Lighting fixtures and controls (excluding fixtures provided by owner)
- Fire alarm system rough-in and coordination with FA contractor
- Low voltage infrastructure (conduit and boxes only)
- Temporary power and lighting during construction
- Final testing, commissioning, and documentation

1.2 Contract Documents

The following documents are incorporated by reference:
- Construction Drawings dated 01/15/2024 (Electrical sheets E-001 through E-089)
- Project Specifications: Sections 260000 - 269999
- Addenda 1, 2, and 3
- RFI responses 001-045 (as of contract date)
- General Conditions (AIA A201-2017)
- Supplementary Conditions

1.3 Exclusions

This subcontract explicitly EXCLUDES:
- Photovoltaic (solar) system installation
- Electric vehicle charging stations (separate contract)
- Audio/visual systems
- Specialty lighting in common areas (by others)
- Utility company service entrance (by utility)

================================================================================
ARTICLE 3: TIME OF PERFORMANCE
================================================================================

3.1 Project Schedule

Subcontractor shall perform work in accordance with:
- Notice to Proceed: March 1, 2024
- Underground rough-in complete: April 15, 2024
- Vertical rough-in complete: September 30, 2024
- Finish work complete: January 15, 2025
- Substantial Completion: February 28, 2025
- Final Completion: March 31, 2025

3.2 Coordination Requirements

Subcontractor shall:
- Attend weekly coordination meetings (Tuesdays, 10:00 AM)
- Provide 4-week look-ahead schedules
- Coordinate with mechanical contractor for equipment connections
- Complete 3D clash detection review within 5 days of model updates

3.3 Liquidated Damages

If Subcontractor fails to achieve milestones due to its own actions:
- Substantial Completion delay: $5,000 per calendar day
- Final Completion delay: $2,500 per calendar day

These damages are not a penalty but represent a reasonable estimate of 
Contractor's actual damages from delay.

3.4 Acceleration

If directed to accelerate, Subcontractor shall be entitled to:
- Actual increased labor costs (with backup documentation)
- Premium time at 1.5x base rate (not to exceed 60 hours/week)
- Prior written approval required for all acceleration measures

================================================================================
ARTICLE 4: PAYMENT TERMS
================================================================================

4.1 Progress Payments

Subcontractor shall submit monthly pay applications by the 25th of each month.
Applications shall include:
- Completed AIA G702/G703 forms
- Lien waivers for prior payments (conditional for current)
- Schedule of Values update
- Certified payroll (if applicable)

4.2 Payment Schedule

- Payment due: 30 days after approved pay application
- Retainage: 10% of each payment (reduced to 5% at 50% completion)
- Retainage release: 45 days after final completion and documentation

4.3 Back-Charges

Contractor may back-charge Subcontractor for:
- Cleanup not performed (after 24-hour notice)
- Damage caused to other trades' work
- Safety violations (first offense: warning; second offense: $500 minimum)
- Schedule impacts caused by Subcontractor

All back-charges require written notice and opportunity to cure.

================================================================================
ARTICLE 6: SAFETY AND COMPLIANCE
================================================================================

6.1 Safety Program

Subcontractor shall:
- Comply with all OSHA regulations (29 CFR 1926)
- Provide site-specific safety plan within 10 days of NTP
- Designate a full-time competent person on-site
- Conduct weekly toolbox talks (documented)
- Participate in Contractor's safety incentive program

6.2 Drug-Free Workplace

All Subcontractor personnel are subject to:
- Pre-employment drug screening
- Random drug and alcohol testing
- Post-incident testing
- Reasonable suspicion testing

Positive test results in immediate removal from project.

6.3 Safety Violations

First violation: Written warning, mandatory retraining
Second violation: $500 fine, worker removed for 30 days
Third violation: Worker permanently barred, potential contract termination

================================================================================
ARTICLE 7: WARRANTY
================================================================================

7.1 Standard Warranty

Subcontractor warrants all work for TWO (2) YEARS from date of Substantial 
Completion. Warranty covers:
- Defective materials
- Faulty workmanship
- Non-compliance with specifications
- System performance failures

7.2 Extended Warranties

The following extended warranties apply:
- Lighting controls: 5 years (manufacturer + installation)
- Emergency lighting: 5 years
- Generator/ATS system: 3 years

7.3 Warranty Response

Subcontractor must respond to warranty claims within:
- Emergency (safety/life threatening): 4 hours
- Urgent (business impact): 24 hours
- Non-urgent: 72 hours

================================================================================
ARTICLE 8: CHANGES AND CLAIMS
================================================================================

8.1 Change Order Process

No changes shall be performed without written authorization.
Change order requests must include:
- Detailed scope description
- Labor breakdown (hours by trade)
- Material costs (invoices or quotes)
- Equipment costs
- Overhead and profit (10% maximum)

8.2 Concealed Conditions

Differing site conditions must be reported within 24 hours of discovery.
Failure to provide timely notice waives claims for additional compensation.

8.3 Claims Procedure

Claims must be submitted within 21 days of the event giving rise to the claim.
Claims must include:
- Statement of facts
- Supporting documentation
- Quantum (time and/or cost)
- Reservation of rights

================================================================================
ARTICLE 9: DISPUTE RESOLUTION
================================================================================

9.1 Negotiation

Parties agree to attempt good-faith negotiation before formal proceedings.
Project-level representatives have 15 days to resolve disputes.
Executive-level representatives have an additional 15 days.

9.2 Mediation

If negotiation fails, parties agree to non-binding mediation.
Mediator selected: American Arbitration Association (AAA)
Costs split equally between parties.

9.3 Arbitration

If mediation fails, disputes shall be resolved by binding arbitration.
Venue: Houston, Texas
Rules: AAA Construction Industry Arbitration Rules
Decision final and binding; limited appeal rights per Texas law.

================================================================================
ARTICLE 10: TERMINATION
================================================================================

10.1 Termination for Cause

Contractor may terminate for cause if Subcontractor:
- Fails to provide adequate workforce
- Fails to make timely payments to suppliers or sub-subcontractors
- Violates safety requirements (3 or more violations)
- Becomes insolvent or files bankruptcy
- Abandons the work

Seven (7) days written notice and opportunity to cure required.

10.2 Termination for Convenience

Contractor may terminate for convenience with 14 days notice.
Subcontractor entitled to:
- Payment for work completed
- Demobilization costs
- Reasonable cancellation charges for materials

Subcontractor NOT entitled to lost profits on unperformed work.
